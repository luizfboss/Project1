Android's mascot story

You might know that the operational systemAndroid, owned by Googleis one of the most used operational systems for mobile devices in the world. However, did you know that the mascot has a really curious name and story? Follow this article to learn about this little green robot.

The first version

The first try of creation was in 2007, by Dan Morrill 🔗. He claims that he launchedInkscape 🔗(a free software for image vectorization) and created his own robot version. His goal was to customize the system for his workmates, and there was no company request to create a new mascot.


The first creepy version was called Dandroids.

A new mascot shows up

The idea of having a mascot had been maturing and the mission was given to a professional of this field. The russian illustrator Irina Blok 🔗, a Google, was responsible for the creation of a more friendly version of this robot.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything in a more minimalist way. The drawing also had to have a quick recognition. TheBugdroid was created.

The main inspiration for the newBugdroidsketches came from those stickmans from restroom doors, in order to identify the genders for each restroom. It is said that the artist was creating on your desk atGoogleand looked to the restrooms and the recognition was immediate: simple and clean.

Do you want to know more?

Other curious subjects related to the Android is that every version always was renamed in honor to a candy, in alphabetical order from 1.5 version to 9.0 version.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lolipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Piev
